I am passionate about geospatial & web technologies

As a member of the Foothills Facility for Remote Sensing and GIScience (F3GISci) research group, I am working on Heat Energy Assessment Technologies (HEAT) — the MIT award winning project from University of Calgary. It has been featured in the Canadian Geographic, CBC, Calgary Herald, and more. My role is prototype and develop user–centric components seen on the project’s web mapping application to increase client engagement and enhance their experience. I am also partly responsible for creating various cartographic products such as digital maps representing waste heat leaving homes, communities, and cities using very high resolution airborne thermal imagery and large–scale GIS datasets.

Additionally, I am an MSc Candidate in the Department of Geography at the University of Calgary with research interests in geospatial technologies such as GIS, remote sensing, and the geospatial web. I believe in buildings tools that can be used for the greater good by citizens. I am motivated by the open data and energy efficiency movement as they have created new opportunities for collaboration and dialogue between people. For my thesis, I am developing a volunteered geographic information (VGI) system as part of the HEAT project to showcase a home’s energy efficiency record over time. With the help of local residents, this VGI system will be used to promote green real–estate and support residential energy efficiency in Calgary.

I have a keen eye for visual design

Cartography isn’t the only domain where I get to showcase my appreciation for aesthetic details. I became involved with visual design such as branding/identity, typography, posters, and other miscellaneous graphics at an early age. As a result, in 2004 I launched XEENAT, which has been my online portfolio featuring selected projects I have worked on for various satisfied clients ranging from non-profit organizations to musicians.

I am fascinated by simplicity of shapes, contrasting and vibrant colour palettes, and sharp angles. With each project, my goal is to ensure balance, harmony, and purity in design to leave a long-lasting effect. All of these qualities lead to simplicity, which is reflected in the work I do and appreciated by my clients. XEENAT is also a medium through which I share self initiated projects that force me to think differently and creatively. Creativity has enhanced my analytical skills by empowering me with the ability to approach each problem differently. This allows me to deliver innovative and cost-effective solutions to real world issues. Moreoever, each personal project provides me with the opportunity to constantly challenge myself in order to produce the highest quality of work, and to keep raising the bar.
